Interpretation of Results

Conclusion

Our results show that there is an efficient use to technology to improve the grocery shopping experience of customers in terms of the queueing and payment processes.
More shoppers with baskets should be allocated to the self checkout queues whenever possible to minimise queueing and service times.

Our results are not surprising to us as customers with baskets (with lesser items) are only allowed to go to the self checkout queue. Also, self checkout servers take a shorter service time
than cashiers would probably be due to the fact that they handle only cashless payments while cashiers are required to handle cash on some transactions, which may result in a longer service time due to the manual processing required.

However, there are still some improvements that can be done for this simulation.



Improvement 1: Add more cashier and self checkout servers

This simulation was based on the context of a small-scale supermarket with 1 cashier counter and 2 self checkout servers.
This may not be accurate as most supermarkets have more than 1 cashier counters as well as more than 2 self checkout servers (e.g. supermarkets at common heartland malls).
One improvement that can be made for this simulation would be to include more servers -- both cashier counters and self checkout servers, in order to have a more realistic simulation of supermarkets.


Improvement 2: Consider amount of groceries purchased


Although this simulation differentiates between individuals that use trolleys and baskets, it does not actually factor in the amount of groceries bought.
For example, shoppers who use baskets tend to purchase a smaller amount of groceries as compared to shoppers who purchase a larger amount of groceries, who tend to use trolleys.
This would naturally mean that shoppers who use baskets would generally have a shorter service time as compared to those who use trolleys.
However, in the event that customers with a large amount of groceries use a basket while customers with a small amount of groceries use a trolley, it is no longer safe to assume that the service time
pattern remains. Therefore, we propose that an additional variable, “Amount of Groceries Purchased” to be added into future analysis to obtain more accurate queueing statistics.
